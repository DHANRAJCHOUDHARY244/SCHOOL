Vysya Limelight Awards (VLA) is the brainchild of Siva Kumar Emmadi aka Emmadi, a Digital entrepreneur. Emmadi is also the Founder of Vysya Business Network (VBN) besides being an active member of the Vysya community. The genesis of the idea lies in the thought that all other communities were fusing their energies for the development of their own communities whereas the Vysya community was doing its own bit, albeit in a limited way. Emmadi felt that the Vysya community also needed to come together more effectively and efficiently.

While more than 150-200 Vysya organisations exist and each one of them is working hard towards their own set objectives and goals there was not a single Association or Club that was formally celebrating the achievements of Vysyas in the country nor were they formally recognising their professional successes. Most of these entities were focusing on social and cultural spaces and very few were focussing on emerging technologies and businesses and how Vysyas can leverage them. Vysya's as known are mainly traders, businessmen, produced and contributed some of the best visionaries to the country. It was Mahatama Gandhi, a Vysya, who led India to Independence. He was famous for being a man who fought in a non-violence way to accomplish his goals. He is the communicator of this century. Bapu was the leader of the world leaders is the Son of this great community. VLA4M-2023

NOMINATION CRITERIA

The VLA4M are open to any Vysya Men born in Telugu States or Living anywhere and Born anywhere and Living in Telugu States. Only adults who know the young person in a professional capacity can nominate for a VLA4M Award. VLA4M core team family members cannot make nominations. There is no limit to the number of nominations that can be made by an individual. Nominees may only be shortlisted in one main category. There is no limit to the number of nominations an individual can submit for The VLA4M Award. Please ensure that you read the Nomination Guidelines before nominating, to check that your entry complies with the criteria.

The VLA have the right to withdraw a nomination if it becomes clear that the nomination has been made by a family member or the nominee does not meet the required reputation, service length, or evidence supporting the nomination is found to be false or written by the nominee.

If during the course of the entry and judging period the nominator’s contact details changes, or the nominator any reason, the nominator is responsible for informing The VLA of these contact changes.

Where a nominee for Award categories is under 18 years of age, the nominee’s Parent or Legal Guardian must provide consent. Nomination forms sent by post must be signed by the nominee’s Parent or Legal Guardian and online nominations must confirm that Parent or Legal Guardian consent has been given.

VLA4M-2023

SUBMISSION & DEADLINE

Entries are accepted only via The VLA nomination centre website vysyalimelightawards.com, Any entries emailed, posted or faxed will not be accepted. The VLA accepts no responsibility for failure to submit entries by the stated deadline or for incomplete entries or those lost due to problems with the internet. Proof of completion is not proof of delivery. All nominations must be received 11:59pm on 5 November 2022 for the Awards year 2022. Entries received after the closing date will not be accepted, nor will any that are incomplete on this date. Nominators may be asked to provide additional evidence or materials after submission.

Nomination forms – including personal information – should be completed in full.

Nominations will be judged only on the information supplied and their contribution to the category criteria.

All achievements should be for the period 5th November 2022
